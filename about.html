About me

Who Am I?

I am a third-year student at the University of Virginia School of Engineering and Applied Science where I am pursuing a B.S in Computer Science with a minor in Engineering Business. I am an undergraduate researcher at UVA under Professor Farzad Farnoud in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department, where my team and I are researching Smooth Compression, a topic of Information Theory.

Outside of school and research I enjoy working out, music (I play electric and bass guitar), traveling, and spending time with loved ones. I am also very interested in finance, specifically investing and trading. To further my understanding and appreciation for these topics, I have been reading market research, The Wall Street Journal, and have especially enjoyed Ernest Chan's books Quantitative Trading and Algorithmic Trading. Dr. Chan's books have been a tremendous resource for my trade execution system project.
